# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in CortexAgent a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, technically rigorous, and respectful community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- showing empathy and respect toward other people
- giving and receiving constructive feedback
- discussing technical disagreements with evidence rather than hostility
- taking responsibility for mistakes and correcting them
- focusing on what is best for the project and its users

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, or demeaning language
- personal attacks or insults
- trolling, deliberate disruption, or bad-faith argumentation
- publishing private information without explicit permission
- sexualized language or imagery
- any other conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior. They may take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples include using an official project email address, posting from an official social media account, or acting as a designated representative at an online or offline event.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the project maintainer through GitHub issues or direct repository contact channels when available. Include enough context for the report to be actionable.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The maintainer will respect the privacy and security of the reporter to the extent reasonably possible.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may use the following guidelines when determining consequences:

### 1. Correction

Used for community-impacting behavior that is inappropriate but not malicious. A private, written warning may be issued with a request for clarification or apology.

### 2. Warning

Used for a single incident or pattern of inappropriate behavior. A warning may include consequences for continued behavior.

### 3. Temporary Ban

Used for serious misconduct or continued inappropriate behavior after a warning. This may include a temporary ban from interaction in project spaces.

### 4. Permanent Ban

Used for sustained harassment, threats, discrimination, or other serious misconduct. This may include permanent removal from project interaction spaces.
